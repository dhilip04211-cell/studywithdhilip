I SECTION I · Power Plant Cycle (Q1–Q29)
Q1
The frequency of emf generation in India is:
DSSSB JE 2021
C — 50 Hz. India's standard grid frequency is 50 Hz. USA uses 60 Hz. This is standardised across all power generation equipment in India.
Q2
What is the aim of all energy conservation techniques?
UPPCL JE 2021
D — Reduce energy losses. The primary aim of all energy conservation techniques is to minimize energy losses during generation, transmission and utilisation of electrical energy.
Q3
Base load of a power station stands for?
SSC JE 2015
A — 12–24 hours/day. Base load is the minimum level of demand on an electrical supply system over 24 hours. Base load plants generate dependable power consistently to meet continuous demand.
Q4
Which of the following power plants employs the Rankine cycle?
SSC JE 2019
C — Thermal power plant. The Rankine cycle is widely used in coal/thermal power plants. Fuel produces heat → water converts to steam → steam expands through turbine. Efficiency ≈ 25–30%.
Q5
Which of them is a peak load station?
UPSSSC JE 2015
D — All of them. Peak load plants include: diesel stations, small capacity thermal plants, run-off river plants. Base load plants: large thermal, hydro, nuclear.
Q6
Gain in kinetic energy is equal to:
UPPCL JE 2018
C — Loss in P.E – work against friction. From energy conservation: Gain in KE = Loss in PE − Work done against friction.
Q7
A generating station having high investment cost and low operating cost is usually operated as a:
BWSSB 2017
B — Base load station. High capital + low running cost plants must run continuously (24 hrs) to recover investment. Requirements: low operating cost, continuous operation, low maintenance cost.
Q8
Power generation of thermal power plant is based on:
UPPCL JE 2016
A — Rankine Cycle. The Rankine cycle is an idealized thermodynamic cycle converting heat into mechanical work via phase change of water/steam.
Q9
Which power plant may take several days to start up and shut down?
GETCO JE 2015
B — Base load. Large coal and nuclear plants (base load) don't change output quickly. Due to thermal constraints they take several days to start/stop. Peak load plants start in minutes.
Q10
Match: Base load = Nuclear power plant; Peak load = Diesel + Gas power plant. Correct answer:
HPSSC JE 2021
A — Nuclear→Base load (slow response, cannot start/stop quickly). Diesel & Gas→Peak load (quick start/stop). Thermal also base load; Wind/Solar are nature-dependent.
Q11
Which of the following are the main parts of a power system?
ESE 2020
D — All three. A complete power system = Generating stations (source) + Transmission system (HV long distance) + Distribution networks (to consumers).
Q12
Power plants using coal work on which cycle?
WBPSC SAE 2003
D — Rankine cycle. Steam power stations convert coal heat energy into electrical energy and work on the Rankine cycle. (Brayton = Gas turbine; Otto = Petrol engine)
Q13
Which of the following is usually NOT a generating voltage?
Mizoram PSC 2016
B — 9.9 kV. Standard generating voltages: 6.6 kV, 11 kV, 13.2 kV, 33 kV. HV transmission: 220/132/66 kV. Primary distribution: 11/33 kV. 9.9 kV is not standard.
Q14
Steam engine is based on which thermodynamic cycle?
UPPCL JE 2018
A — Rankine cycle. Steam engines operate on the Rankine cycle — applicable only to steam power plants. It converts heat to useful work using liquid-to-vapor phase change.
Q15
The biggest unit size of steam plant in India is:
UPRVUNL JE 2016
D — 500 MW. The biggest steam plant unit in India is 500 MW, located at Anpara, Uttar Pradesh.
Q16
Which of the following is NOT a type of generating station?
Nagaland PSC 2017
D — Atmospheric. Thermal, Nuclear, Hydro are conventional generating stations. 'Atmospheric' is not a recognized type of power generating station.
Q17
Super thermal power stations are installed at all EXCEPT:
Nagaland PSC 2016
C — Narora. Super thermal power stations are at Singrauli, Farraka, Ramagundam. Narora (Bulandshahr, UP) hosts a Nuclear Power Plant, not a super thermal station.
Q18
If normal frequency is 50 Hz and operated at 53 Hz, which equipment is most adversely affected?
Nagaland PSC 2016
C — Turbine. Turbines are speed-sensitive. Ns = 120f/P, so higher frequency = higher speed. At 53 Hz instead of 50 Hz, turbine speed exceeds rated value causing mechanical stress.
Q19
The generation voltage is usually:
BWSSB 2017
A — 11 kV to 33 kV. Synchronous generators produce electricity at 11–33 kV. Step-up transformers then raise it to 132–400 kV for long-distance transmission.
Q20
Which of the following is NOT a peak load power plant?
UPPCL JE 2019
D — Thermal power plant. Thermal plants are BASE load plants (high capacity, continuous). Peak load plants (quick start/stop): Diesel, Gas, Pumped storage hydro.
Q21
Which type of Hydel power plants can be used as both base and peak load plants?
UPPCL JE 2016
C — Storage hydro type plants. Four types: Run-off river, Pumped-storage, Storage, Offshore. Storage hydro plants serve as both base and peak load plants due to reservoir flexibility.
Q22
Generators for peak load plants are usually designed for maximum efficiency at:
PGCIL 2018
D — 50 to 75% full load. Peak load generators (pumped storage, diesel-electric) are designed for maximum efficiency at 50–75% of full load, since peak load is intermittent.
Q23
Which type of power plant is NOT used as a base load power plant?
GPSC AAE 2021
C — Pumped storage power plant. Pumped storage plants are PEAK load plants — they pump water up during low demand and release it for generation during high demand (peak) periods.
Q24
Which of the following plants is suitable for peak load?
JKSSB JE 2014
A — Diesel engine plant. Peak load plants: Diesel engine, Solar, Gas, Wind turbine — they start quickly. Steam and Nuclear are base load; standard Hydro can be both.
Q25
The unvarying load which occurs almost the whole day on the station is known as:
DSSSB JE 2021
C — Base load. The minimum continuous load on any substation for the whole day (24 hours) is known as base load. It remains approximately constant throughout the day.
Q26
Which power station is mainly used to cover peak load on the system?
ESE 2010
D — Pumped storage hydro power plant. Base load: Nuclear, Coal, Reservoir Hydro, Geothermal. Peak load: Gas, Diesel, Pumped storage hydro. Pumped storage has quick response to peak demand.
Q27
Coal fired steam: load factor 90%, 400 MW, coal heat 2 kWh/kg, efficiency 60%, train=900 metric tons. How many trainloads daily?
BIS TA 2020
C — 8 trainloads. Energy/day = 400x24=9600 MWh. Input=9600/0.6=16000 MWh. Coal=16000x1000/2=8000 metric tons. Trainloads=8000/900=8.88≈8.
Q28
At times of peak loads, a power system needs:
SSC JE 2011
A — Injection of lagging VAR's. Peak loads are mainly lagging power factor loads (motors, induction loads). So the system needs lagging VARs (reactive power) to support voltage during peak demand.
Q29
Which is an example of conversion of heat energy to mechanical energy?
UPPCL JE 2021
A — Steam engine. Coal burns → water vaporises → hot steam drives turbine → mechanical work. Piezoelectric = mechanical→electric; Photosynthesis = light→chemical energy.
II SECTION II · Thermal Power Plant (Q30–Q141)
Q30
In a thermal power plant, Economiser is used to heat:
PSTCL AE 2021
C — Feed Water. Economiser heats feed water using flue gas, increasing boiler thermal efficiency by 10–12% and saving fuel by 5–15%. It extends boiler life and increases boiler capacity.
Q31
The problematic pollutants in emission of coal based generating plants are:
DGVCL JE 2016
D — All of the above. Coal combustion emits: SO₂, SO₃, NOx, CO, particulate matter, mercury (Hg), trace metals, and radioactive nucleotides — all harmful environmental pollutants.
Q32
The part of steam power plant that extracts heat from flue gases before applying to boiler is:
DMRC JE 2018
D — Economiser. It is a mechanical device that preheats feed water using heat from flue gases, reducing energy consumption and acting as a heat exchanger.
Q33
Which heat exchanger raises steam temperature from saturated to superheated level?
PSTCL AE 2021
C — Superheater converts saturated/wet steam into superheated dry steam. Air preheater: recovers heat from flue gas for combustion air. Economizer: preheats feed water.
Q34
Which treatment for water removes non-carbonate hardness?
APTRANSCO 2012
B — Soda treatment. Adding lime (CaO) and soda (Na₂CO₃) precipitates calcium as carbonate and magnesium as hydroxide, removing hardness. Lime softening removes Ca/Mg ions (permanent hardness).
Q35
The hoppers in electrostatic precipitator are generally _____ in shape:
APTRANSCO 2012
B — Pyramidal. Hoppers are large pyramidal or cone-shaped containers. In ESP systems, fly ash attracted by high-voltage electrodes (30,000–60,000 V) falls into these pyramidal hoppers.
Q36
The purpose of cooling tower is:
APTRANSCO 2012
D — Cool steam in the condenser. Cooling tower converts hot water/steam from condenser into cool water for reuse in the plant cycle.
Q37
Air pollution due to smoke around thermal power station can be reduced by:
ESE 2016
D — Electrostatic precipitator. ESP is the most efficient device for capturing fly ash particles from flue gases, dramatically reducing air pollution. Efficiency up to 99.5%.
Q38
Which of the following is a conventional source of electricity generation?
EESL 2020
A — Thermal. Conventional (non-renewable): Coal, Petroleum, Natural gas, Thermal, Nuclear. Non-conventional (renewable): Bio energy, Solar, Wind, Tidal, Geothermal.
Q39
In thermal power station, heat balance in descending order: (1)Work done (2)Friction loss (3)Heat to circulating water (4)Heat in condensate — correct order:
ESE 2011
C — 3, 1, 4, 2. Descending: Heat to circulating water (largest) → Work done (thermal efficiency) → Heat in condensate → Friction and windage loss (smallest). Shows why thermal efficiency is low.
Q40
For 3-element feed water control, water level measurements ensure:
ESE 2010
C — Violate specified upper and lower limits. 3-element control (water level + steam flow + feed water flow) ensures drum water stays within safe upper AND lower limits simultaneously.
Q41
Maximum efficiency of modern coal-fired steam plants is restricted to about 0.35 mainly because of:
Mizoram PSC 2019
D — High energy loss from turbine exhaust to condenser. This unavoidable heat rejection in the condenser (Rankine cycle limitation) is why thermal efficiency is capped at ~35%.
Q42
Major share of power in India is produced through:
Mizoram PSC 2019
A — Thermal power plants (63%). India mix: Thermal 63%, Hydro 13%, Non-conventional 12%, Gas 7%, Nuclear 2%, Diesel 1%.
Q43
The economiser of steam turbine system is located in:
PSPCL JE 2019
B — Feeding water circuit. Economiser is a bundle of horizontal tubes in feed water circuit. Feed water absorbs residual heat from exhaust flue gases on its way to the boiler.
Q44
Which generating power station requires lot of time for starting?
PGCIL 2018
A — Steam power station. Steam plants are quasi-static — many energy conversion processes must stabilise (boiler warm-up, steam pressure building) before full power is possible.
Q45
The function of condenser in thermal power plant is:
SSC JE 2019
B — Condense used steam into water. Condenser converts exhaust steam from turbine back into water (condensate) for reuse as feed water in the boiler, completing the Rankine cycle.
Q46
_____ is NOT a component in a thermal power plant:
SSC JE 2019
C — Reactor. Nuclear reactor is the main part of a NUCLEAR power plant. Chimney, boiler, and economiser are all components of thermal (steam) power plants.
Q47
Name the generating station where electrical energy is generated through steam:
SSC JE 2018
A — Thermal power station. Thermal stations use heat from burning coal to generate steam, which drives turbines to produce electrical energy. Thermal efficiency ≈ 30%.
Q48
Which type of fan is used between dust collector and chimney in thermal power plant?
SSC JE 2018
B — Induced draft fan. ID fan is placed between dust collector (ESP) and chimney. It creates vacuum/negative pressure to pull flue gases through the system and expel via chimney.
Q49
The air preheater in thermal power plant is called as:
SSC JE 2018
B — Efficiency increasing equipment. Air preheater heats incoming combustion air using flue gases, recovering waste heat and increasing thermal efficiency by reducing fuel needed.
Q50
Forced draft fan handles which type of air?
SSC JE 2018
A — Cold air. FD (forced draft) fan pushes cold fresh air into the boiler for combustion. ID (induced draft) fan handles hot flue gases on the exit side. FD fan pressure = positive; ID = negative.
Q51
The overall efficiency of a steam power plant is about:
UPPCL JE 2019
B — 29%. Plant efficiencies: Steam 29%, Nuclear 30–37%, Hydro 85%, Diesel 42%, Gas 40%. Steam is lowest due to large condenser heat rejection losses.
Q52
100 MW station, thermal eff 30%, electrical eff 92%. Find overall efficiency:
UPPCL JE 2019
B — 27.6%. Overall = Thermal × Electrical = (30/100)×(92/100)×100 = 27.6%
Q53
What is the name of the power generating station which uses coal as a fuel?
SSC JE 2020
B — Thermal power station. A thermal/steam power station converts coal combustion heat energy into electrical energy. It works on the Rankine cycle.
Q54
In a thermal power plant, feed water heaters, super heaters and air preheaters are mainly used to:
UPSSSC JE 2015
C — Increase the efficiency of the plant. All three devices recover waste heat at various stages, reducing fuel consumption and improving overall plant efficiency significantly.
Q55
Coal used in power plant is also known as:
UPSSSC JE 2015
A — Steam coal. Coal used in thermal power plants is called steam coal. For efficient combustion, it is pulverized into fine powder — called pulverized coal — before burning.
Q56
Binary vapour cycles are used to:
UPPCL JE 2018
B — Increase the efficiency of the plant. A binary vapour cycle combines two thermodynamic cycles (one in high-temp, one in lower-temp region) to better utilise heat across temperature ranges.
Q57
The draught produced by chimney — if chimney gas temperature increases, draught:
UPPCL JE 2018
B — Increases. Draught = pressure difference due to density difference between hot gas inside and cold air outside chimney. Higher temperature → lower gas density → stronger draught.
Q58
The overall efficiency of steam power plant is defined as:
SSC JE 2020
B — Heat equivalent of electrical output / heat of combustion of coal. This is the true measure of station performance. Typical efficiency = 33–48%.
Q59
The prime mover used in thermal power plant is:
SSC JE 2020
B — Steam turbine. Steam turbines are the prime movers in thermal power plants. They extract thermal energy from pressurized steam and convert it to mechanical work on a rotating shaft.
Q60
In a steam power plant, the condensate from condenser is used:
SSC JE 2020
B — As feed water to the boiler. Condensate pump → low pressure feed water heater → high pressure heater → back to boiler. This closed cycle minimizes water consumption.
Q61
Steam power station: thermal efficiency 30%, electrical efficiency 90%. Overall efficiency:
SSC JE 2020
A — 27%. Overall = Thermal × Electrical = 0.30 × 0.90 = 0.27 = 27%
Q62
A modern coal-fired thermal power station consumes about _____ of its power for auxiliaries:
SSC JE 2020
B — 10%. Thermal power stations consume about 10% of generated power for auxiliary equipment (fans, pumps, coal handling, lighting, etc.). Called station service/auxiliary power.
Q63
Which of the following fuels is used as a fossil fuel in electric power generation?
SSC JE 2020
A — Coal. Coal is a hard fossil fuel (mostly carbon + hydrogen, sulphur, oxygen, nitrogen). Solar, Wind, Water are renewable non-fossil energy sources.
Q64
Which of the following is a conventional method of electrical energy generation?
PGCIL 2018
C — Thermal power plant. Conventional (non-renewable): Thermal, Nuclear, Large Hydro. Non-conventional (renewable): Solar PV, Wind, Fuel cells, Tidal, Geothermal, Biomass.
Q65
Which device is suitable for removal of gaseous pollutants?
RRB JE 2019
C — Wet collector (scrubber). Scrubbers remove gaseous pollutants (SO₂, HCl, etc.) from exhaust gases using a scrubbing liquid. ESP removes particulate matter; cyclone removes large particles.
Q66
The term 'Live storage of coal' in a power plant relates to:
BSPHCL JE 2019
D — Storage sufficient for 24-hour demand. Live storage is the working coal stock that feeds the plant directly, designed to meet the plant's 24-hour coal requirement.
Q67
Which cycle is practically suitable for coal-fired thermal power plant?
BSPHCL JE 2019
D — Rankine cycle only. Thermal stations work on the Rankine cycle (2 isothermal + 2 adiabatic + 1 isobaric processes). Efficiency of Rankine cycle = 30–45%.
Q68
Natural draught in a steam power plant is produced by:
UPPCL JE 2021
C — Chimneys. Natural (chimney) draught is created by density difference between hot flue gases inside and cold air outside the chimney. No mechanical fan needed — purely thermal buoyancy.
Q69
Which power plant has least efficiency?
RSMSSB JEN 2020
A — Steam Power Plant (29%). Efficiency: Hydro 85%, Diesel 42%, Nuclear 30–37%, Steam 29%. Steam has the lowest due to large unavoidable heat rejection in the condenser.
Q70
The optimal scheduling problem in case of a thermal plant can be solved at any desired instant:
SSC JE 2017
B — Without reference to operation at other times. Thermal plant scheduling is instantaneous — optimal output at any moment is determined solely from current incremental costs.
Q71
Overall efficiency: boiler eff=85%, turbine eff=40%, alternator eff=95%:
SSC JE 2020
B — 32.3%. Overall = ηb × ηt × ηa = 0.85 × 0.40 × 0.95 = 0.323 = 32.3%
Q72
Turbine efficiency of a steam thermal power plant varies between:
SSC JE 2020
C — 25 to 35%. Steam turbine efficiency is 25–35%. Despite modern turbines being 85% efficient internally, thermodynamic cycle limits overall station efficiency to ~29%.
Q73
Superheater is employed to: (1)remove moisture (2)increase vapour temp (3)heat feed water — which is INCORRECT?
UPPCL JE 2016
B — 3 only is incorrect. Superheater: (1) Removes moisture ✓ (2) Increases vapour temperature ✓. Heating feed water is done by the ECONOMISER, NOT the superheater.
Q74
The exact direction of fan for completing the induced draught should be:
UPPCL JE 2016
D — Near the bottom of chimney. ID fan near chimney base pulls flue gases through: boiler → economizer → air preheater → ESP → ID fan → chimney.
Q75
In a steam power plant, the working fluid is:
UPPCL JE 2019
B — Water. Water is the working fluid in steam power plants. Advantages: widely available, inexpensive, high specific heat, non-toxic. It undergoes phase change (water→steam→water) in the Rankine cycle.
Q76
Which of the following is NOT a part of a steam power plant?
UPPCL JE 2019
C — Diffuser is not part of steam power plant. Boiler: generates steam. Superheater: raises steam temperature. Economiser: preheats feed water using flue gas heat.
Q77
In steam electric power plant, which is the pulverised fuel burner?
UPPCL JE 2019
D — Cyclone burner. In steam power plants, cyclone-type burners are used for pulverised coal. Coal is pulverised in ball mills and fired through cyclone burners for efficient combustion.
Q78
Efficiencies: generator=0.97, turbine=0.95, boiler=0.92, cycle=0.42, overall=0.33. What % consumed by auxiliaries?
UPPCL JE 2019
C — 7.32%. 0.33=0.97×0.95×0.92×0.42×ηa → ηa=0.926. Auxiliary = 1–0.926 = 0.0736 ≈ 7.3%.
Q79
The efficiency of a wet electrostatic precipitator is:
UPPCL JE 2019
D — 99.5%. Wet ESP achieves 99.5% efficiency separating fine particles from gas using electrostatic charge (30,000–60,000 V conductors attracting charged particles to collection plates).
Q80
A pulverized coal boiler generates approximately ___fly ash and ___bottom ash:
UPPCL JE 2019
D — 80% fly ash, 20% bottom ash. When pulverized coal burns: 80% of dust becomes fly ash (carried upward with flue gas, collected by ESP) and 20% falls as bottom ash.
Q81
Correct sequence for heat transfer of water in a steam generator:
UPPCL JE 2019
B — Economiser → Evaporator → Superheater. Water preheated in economizer → evaporated in boiler → wet steam dried/superheated in superheater before entering turbine.
Q82
Which impurity causes temporary hardness in feed water?
UPPCL JE 2019
D — Bicarbonates → Temporary hardness (removed by boiling). Sulphates and chlorides of Ca/Mg → Permanent hardness (needs chemical treatment like lime-soda process).
Q83
Correct sequence of flue gas flow in thermal power plant:
UPPCL JE 2019
A — Boiler→Economizer→Air preheater→(ESP)→ID fan→Chimney. Economizer recovers heat to feed water first, then APH recovers heat for combustion air, then ESP removes ash.
Q84
In steam power plant, ___ heats feed water on its way to boiler using flue gas heat:
UPPCL JE 2016
B — Economizer. It is a heat exchanger recovering more heat from flue gases than normal air preheater can. It preheats feed water, reducing fuel needed and improving efficiency.
Q85
Which is NOT a part of steam power plant?
UPPCL JE 2016
D — Surge Chamber. Surge chamber is part of HYDRO power plant — it controls water hammering in penstock. Steam plants include switch yard, ash precipitators, and draught fans.
Q86
The first thermal power plant of India is:
UPPCL JE 2019
D — Hussain Sagar TPS. Located on Hussainsagar lake, Hyderabad, Telangana. Opened in 1920 — India's first thermal power station. Nameplate capacity = 22.5 MW.
Q87
Air forced into tower in mechanical draft cooling towers by using:
UPPCL JE 2019
C — Propellers. Mechanical draft cooling towers use propeller fans to force/induce air through the tower. Natural draft towers use chimney effect from hyperbolic tower height.
Q88
The function of electrostatic precipitator in coal based thermal power plant is to:
UPPCL JE 2019
A — Collect dust from flue gas. ESP removes fly ash from flue gases. Dust-filled gas passes between conductors at 30,000–60,000 V, charged particles collected on grounded plates.
Q89
_____ is present between air-preheater and induced fan in a thermal power plant:
NLC GET 2020
C — Electrostatic precipitator. Gas flow: Boiler→Economizer→Air Preheater→ESP→ID Fan→Chimney. ESP removes particles before gas exits through the fan and chimney.
Q90
The efficiency of modern steam turbines is about:
Punjab Mandi Board JE 2014
B — 85%. Modern steam turbines achieve ~85% efficiency internally. However, overall plant efficiency is less than 40% because boiler and condenser losses are dominant.
Q91
The overall efficiency of thermal station is:
Punjab Mandi Board JE 2014
B — Less than 40%. Modern thermal plants have overall efficiency less than 40%. Component efficiencies: thermodynamic cycle 45%, boiler 85%, steam turbines 85%, generator 98%.
Q92
The main function of economizer of a boiler in a plant is to:
Punjab Mandi Board JE 2014
B — Reduce fuel consumption. By preheating feed water, economizer reduces energy (fuel) needed to convert water to steam. Recovers waste flue gas heat, lowering required firing rate.
Q93
Super heater is used in thermal power plant to:
UJVNL JE 2016
B — Reduce moisture contents of steam. Superheater removes last traces of moisture above saturation temperature, producing dry superheated steam for efficient turbine operation.
Q94
Statements about steam boilers: (1)Quick start/load (2)No joints exposed to flames (3)Can burn low ash coal — all correct?
ESE 2020
D — All three. Good steam boiler qualities: (1) Quick starting/loading (2) No joints exposed to flame (3) Efficient burning including low ash coal (4) Easy cleaning/maintenance.
Q95
Which coal has the highest calorific value?
UPPCL JE 2019
D — Anthracite (7600–7800 kcal/kg). Coal CV ranking: Anthracite > Bituminous > Lignite > Peat. Anthracite (86–92% carbon) is also best for thermal power plants.
Q96
The quality of coal received by cement plants has ash content in range of:
UPPCL JE 2019
B — 30–40 percent. Coal for cement plants typically has 30–40% ash content. High ash coal is acceptable since fly ash is used as a raw material in cement production.
Q97
Heating value of coal is approximately:
Mizoram PSC 2016
C — 5000–6500 kcal/kg. General heating value of power station coal: 5000–6500 kcal/kg. Anthracite: 7600–7800; Bituminous: 4200–7000; Lignite: ~4200.
Q98
Equipment installed in power plants to reduce air pollution due to smoke:
Mizoram PSC 2016
C — Electrostatic precipitators. ESP removes suspended dust particles from gas/exhaust by applying high voltage. Charged plates collect particles, reducing particulate emission by >99%.
Q99
Most generators in thermal power plants run at:
Mizoram PSC 2016
A — 3000 rpm. Ns=120f/P=120×50/2=3000 rpm for 2-pole machines (50 Hz). Thermal/nuclear generators use 2-pole cylindrical rotor turbo-generators. Hydro uses 10–20 poles at 300–600 rpm.
Q100
Synchronous generators used in thermal power plants have:
Mizoram PSC 2015
A — Cylindrical rotors. Thermal generators (turbo-generators) use cylindrical rotors — smaller diameter, large axial length. Hydro generators use salient pole rotors — larger diameter, small axial length.
Q101
Steam station: overall efficiency 20%, 0.5 kg coal/kWh. Calorific value? (1kWh=860 kcal)
PGCIL 2018
D — 8600 kcal/kg. η=860/(coal/kWh × x) → 0.20=860/(0.5×x) → x=860/0.1=8600 kcal/kg.
Q102
Electrostatic precipitator is used for:
RRB Guwahati 2014
A — Particulate emission control. ESP located between ID fan and combustion chamber in thermal plants. Removes particles from gas stream using electrical energy — for controlling particulate matter.
Q103
In steam power stations, the condenser creates a _____ at the exhaust of the turbine:
CPCL JE 2019
D — Very low pressure (vacuum). Condenser creates vacuum at turbine exhaust by condensing steam to water. This large pressure drop across the turbine maximizes turbine work output.
Q104
The advantage of using pulverized fuel include:
UKPSC JE 2013
D — All of the above. Pulverized coal advantages: (1) Higher boiler efficiency (2) Easy and complete combustion (3) Low air requirement (4) Low-grade coal can be burned easily.
Q105
In thermal power plants, the pressure of working fluid cycle is developed by:
UKPSC JE 2013
C — Feed water pump. The feed water pump develops pressure in the working fluid cycle. It pumps condensate from condenser to high pressure for boiler operation — only work input in Rankine cycle.
Q106
The pH value of water used for boiler of thermal power plant is:
UKPSC JE 2013
C — Slightly more than 7 (slightly alkaline). Boiler feed water is kept slightly alkaline to prevent corrosion of boiler tubes. Acidic water causes rapid corrosion; neutral still corrodes; slightly alkaline is optimal.
Q107
The location of steam generating station is determined by:
WBPSC SAE 2000
D — All the above. Steam station siting: (1) Load centre proximity (2) Water availability for cooling (3) Coal delivery infrastructure (4) Ash disposal facility (5) Land availability.
Q108
Which feed water treatment is basically one type of thermal treatment?
SSC JE 2021
B — Deaeration. Uses heat (steam) to remove dissolved oxygen and CO₂ from feed water, preventing corrosion in boiler tubes. It is a thermal treatment. Filtration/sedimentation = physical; coagulation = chemical.
Q109
In a steam power plant, which is NOT an artificial draught?
SSC JE 2021
B — Chimney draught is NOT artificial. Chimney draught = Natural draught (thermal buoyancy, no mechanical equipment). Artificial draughts use fans: Forced, Induced, and Balanced draught.
Q110
Steam station: overall efficiency 25%, 0.5 kg coal/kWh. Calorific value? (1kWh=860 kcal)
SSC JE 2021
D — 6880 kcal/kg. CV = 860/(η × coal/kWh) = 860/(0.25 × 0.5) = 860/0.125 = 6880 kcal/kg.
Q111
Thermal power stations: (i) Pollute atmosphere (ii) Costlier running than hydro (iii) Require larger space than hydro — TRUE/FALSE?
SSC JE 2021
A — (i)True, (ii)True, (iii)FALSE. Thermal plants DO pollute and running cost IS higher than hydro. But thermal plants occupy LESS space than hydro (hydro needs huge reservoir, dam, large land area).
Q112
Steam station data: Max demand=25MW, Load factor=0.4, Boiler eff=85%, Turbine eff=90%. Find thermal efficiency:
SSC JE 2021
A — 76.5%. Thermal efficiency = Boiler efficiency × Turbine efficiency = 0.85 × 0.90 = 0.765 = 76.5%.
